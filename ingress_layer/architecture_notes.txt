1. Ingress Layer
Where everything enters the system

What it is:
The Ingress Layer is the front door.
It accepts:
- Text messages (SMS, WhatsApp, chat)
- Chat logs (web, app, customer support)
- Voice calls (via live audio or recordings -> transcription)

It also captures metadata, such as:
- Timestamp of each message
- Channel (SMS, call, Telegram, etc.)
- Sender identifiers (masked if needed)
- Message order and gaps between messages

Why it matters (hidden reason):
Scams are time-sensitive and channel-specific.
Examples:
- Voice scams escalate faster than text
- WhatsApp scams behave differently from SMS
- Time gaps can signal: Script switching, Human handoff, Bot vs human
Without metadata, you lose behavioral signals.

What breaks if you skip this:
- You canʼt reconstruct the scam timeline
- You canʼt tell if urgency is artificial
- Voice-based scams become impossible to analyze properly

👉 This layer is not “dumb input.” Itʼs evidence collection.
